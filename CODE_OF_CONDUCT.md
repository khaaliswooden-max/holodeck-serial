# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This is a research project. Interactions — in issues, pull requests, and
discussions — are expected to be direct, technically rigorous, and respectful.

Specific expectations:

- **Engage with the work.** Critiques of the benchmark framework, open questions,
  or implementation choices are welcome and encouraged. Adversarial review is
  a feature, not a threat.
- **Cite your claims.** Asserting that a threshold is wrong or a benchmark is
  invalid requires evidence — a citation, a derivation, or experimental data.
  Opinion without basis will not move the conversation forward.
- **Mark your uncertainty.** Use VERIFIED / PLAUSIBLE / SPECULATIVE in
  technical discussion, not just in code. It clarifies the epistemic status
  of your argument and makes disagreement more productive.
- **Be direct.** Unnecessary softening or hedging wastes everyone's time.
  Say what you mean.

## What Is Not Welcome

- Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory language
- Bad-faith contributions designed to introduce parallelism or GPU
  dependencies under the guise of "optimization"
- Plagiarism of research contributions without attribution

## Enforcement

This is a small research project maintained by Zuup Innovation Lab. Issues
will be addressed at maintainer discretion. Repeated violations result in
removal from the project.
